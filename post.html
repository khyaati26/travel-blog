Between its corniches and modern quarter are Portuguese-built forts, blue-domed mosques, royal palaces, and souks, all heavy with the sense of a lost Arabia. Places to Visit

Mutrah Corniche is a promenade stretching for 3 kilometres along the waterfront and is lined with restaurants, cafes, and markets.

Sultan Qaboos Grand Mosque in Muscat is one of the few mosques in Arabia which allows entry for non-Muslim visitors. The mosque is a wonder of modern architecture and is a fusion of Omani, Islamic, and Middle-Eastern architectural styles.

Qurum Beach, at the edge of the Qurum Nature Reserve, stretches for four kilometres along the Muscat coastline, in the heart of Muscat city. The clean waters of the Gulf of Oman are perfect for rejuvenation and relaxation, away from the hustle and bustle of the city life.

Qantab beach of Qantab village in Muscat, in northeastern Oman, is a popular tourist destination.

Best place to shop, City Centre Muscat, a mall located on Sultan Qaboos Road, Seeb.

Mutrah Souq one of the oldest markets in the Arab world. This antiquity has perhaps increased its beauty, magic and allure. I could see many Indian business man displaying Omani and Indian craft especially craft from Kashmir and Rajasthan. You can use bargaining tool here.

Marina Bandar Al Rowdha is Muscat's original marina located at the foot of the Al Hajar Mountains giving berth holders access to the best cruising ground close to the the city. Got glimpse of Whale while sailing in the midst of Gulf Ocean.
